# Motivation: Why Open Source?

### **Air Quality is a Universal Concern**
Air pollution is an invisible crisis impacting millions worldwide. Yet, access to **accurate, real-time Air Quality Index (AQI) data** is scarce. Existing monitoring solutions are expensive, limited in coverage, and leave many communities uninformed about the air they breathe.

### **The Problem with Existing Solutions**
1. **Data Gaps & Exclusion:** Current AQI monitoring networks do not cover all locations, leading to inaccurate and incomplete air quality assessments.
2. **High Cost:** Commercial AQI monitors cost **$400 or more**, making them unattainable for many individuals and communities.
3. **Lack of Public Awareness:** Without easy access to real-time air quality data, people remain unaware of the health risks posed by polluted air.
4. **Severe Health Impacts:** Bangladesh frequently experiences **record-breaking AQI levels**, causing respiratory diseases, cardiovascular conditions, and long-term health damage. Clean air is a fundamental right.

### **Why OpenAQI?**
1. **Affordable & Accessible Monitoring:** We aim to create low-cost, open-source AQI devices, ensuring that everyone—regardless of income—can monitor their air quality.
2. **Empowering Citizen Science:** OpenAQI is a collective effort where **more contributors = more data = greater accuracy** in AQI assessments.
3. **First Fully Open-Source AQI Project:** No global initiative currently offers an open-source AQI hardware, software, and data ecosystem. We are changing that.
4. **Community-Driven, Scalable Solution:** By crowdsourcing air quality data, we can generate **accurate, localized AQI insights** that support better public health policies and environmental actions.
5. **Actionable Insights for Change:** When people have access to **real-time AQI data**, they can take informed actions—whether it’s wearing masks, using air purifiers, or advocating for cleaner air policies.

### **Join the Movement**
Clean air data should be a **public resource, not a privilege**. OpenAQI invites developers, researchers, activists, and citizens to **collaborate, innovate, and contribute** to a global, open-source air quality monitoring network.

**Together, we can make AQI data open, accessible, and actionable—for a healthier future.**
